# 2024 Mid-Year City Progress – Data Analysis

![City Progress Dashboard](https://github.com/user-attachments/assets/b55333ed-d30b-48f3-8b8d-a10c859c5f69)

## Project Overview

This project presents an independent analysis of Calgary's 2024 Mid-Year City Progress, focusing on municipal budget performance measures and critical city-wide issues. Leveraging open data from the City of Calgary, the study investigates the city’s development status, the handling of social equity, climate, transit, and housing challenges, and compares progress between 2023, 2024, and future initiatives.

> **Note:** This analysis is for educational purposes and uses only publicly available datasets. It is not affiliated with or endorsed by the City of Calgary.

---

## Research Questions

- **What is the status of Calgary’s development as of 2024?**
- **How are social equity, climate, transit, and housing issues being dealt with?**
- **How does data from 2023 compare to 2024 and the city’s future plans?**

## Analytical Approach & Tools

1. **Data Acquisition:**  
   - Downloaded the 2024 Mid-Year Progress dataset in CSV format.
   - Supplemented findings with spring and fall 2024 Calgary resident surveys.

2. **Database Management:**  
   - Established a SQL database in **Microsoft SQL Server Management Studio** to efficiently query and aggregate data.

3. **Data Transformation & Visualization:**  
   - Imported and cleaned data in **Power BI** (converted strings to integers, calculated performance measures).
   - Developed an interactive dashboard highlighting trends and focus areas.
   - Utilized **Microsoft Excel** and **Google Slides** for reporting and presentation.

---

## Key Insights

- **Performance Measures:**  
  - 58.42% of performance measures are on track.
  - 8.59% face setbacks due to various challenges.
  - 32.99% remain unreported, mostly due to data limitations.

- **Critical Focus Areas:**  
  - Analysis centers on **social equity, climate, transit, and housing**.
  - Social equity initiatives receive the strongest emphasis in city reporting, though this may not reflect citizen priorities.

- **2023 vs. 2024 Trends:**  
  - Substantial decrease in reported service values compared to 2023, indicating stalled or delayed initiatives.
  - The city is strategizing for increased investment in transit, water/recycling, and affordable housing for future years.

- **Data Limitations:**  
  - The analysis is restricted to public data; some metrics are incomplete or outdated.
  - Upcoming 2025 reports are expected to provide fuller insights.

---

## Impact 

- **Actionable Insights:**  
  - Highlighted urgent needs in public transit, water/recycling services, and affordable housing.
  - Identified the gap between council priorities and resident concerns.
  - Informed future strategies for service value increases in key sectors.
